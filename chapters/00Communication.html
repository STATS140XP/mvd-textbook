Communication

“First learn the meaning of what you say, and then speak.”
— Epictetus

A General Definition of Communication

Let us start with the basics: what is communication in the context of a consulting team?

A working definition - the exchange of knowledge, information or news specifically as it relates to a consulting project.

Relationship of Communication with Empathy

We believe that successful consulting teams are able to connect with each other and with the client on an emotional and cognitive level. Empathy allows a person to better understand another’s feelings and thoughts which will in turn shape a message. A message which resonates with the recipient (a teammate, a client) helps to build stronger relationships and strengthens the dialogue.

Relationship of Communication with Ethics

It may seem artificial to separate the discussion of ethics and empathy, but having empathy does not guarantee ethical behavior, and a person can behave ethically without possessing empathy Unethical communication can damage trust within a team and with a clients. In this text, there is an entire chapter dedicated to Ethics in consulting, but within the context of communication, we are able to identify key aspects which are utterly destructive to within team or in team-client relations:

  • Deception - a team may give a deceptive member a “second chance" but deceiving a client can destroy the whole endeavor.

  • Withholding information - keeping vital information from a client or from other team members

  • Ambiguity - the intentional use of ambiguous language is a form of deception

  • Gossip and other personal attacks - choosing to label another as “lazy" when they fail to meet a deadline or label a client as”demanding" may cause irreparable harm

  • Violating Confidentiality - whether this involves a private conversation or a non-disclosure agreement, using communication in this manner will destroy a team and expose the enterprise to legal action.

Communication Strategies for Consulting Teams

There are a variety of communication strategies that consulting teams can employ. For example:

  • Active listening - always focus on the understanding the speaker before responding. We see this when clients explain their problem for the first time or when team members are sharing their findings or concerns.

  • MECE principle (mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive) - applying it to break down a client’s problems into clear separate analyses that be distributed to consulting team members.

  • Storytelling - when constructing deliverables for the client, information should be organized in a story format, e.g., introduction to climax and then resolution.

  • Data Visualization - always employ charts and graphics to represent data and findings.

  • Open Access - encourage team members to share ideas thoughts and concerns. Do this to ensure trust and collaboration within the team.

Barriers to Effective Communication and Overcoming Them

Cultural/Linguistic barriers

Cultural barriers, remote communication challenges, or linguistic misunderstandings.

  • Proactively create opportunities for teammates of different cultures to meet and interact

  • Encourage team members to share about their backgrounds and experiences towards the goal of building trust, respect and understanding.

  • Rotate meeting times to accommodate everyone

  • Use asynchronous communication

  • Keep communication simple

  • Utilize visualizations if possible

Unclear Expectations

From the start of a project, its goals, deliverables, deadlines and roles should be clearly understood. In an imperfect world, this does not always happen. We suggest some time tested strategies for remedying the situation:

  • Document everything

  • Meet regularly

  • Provide or insist on feedback regularly

  • Use communication technology - examples Slate, GitHub

Exercises

Small Group

Two truths and a lie - Active listening and communication

If there are enough participants, randomly assign them to teams of size 4 or so. . Each team member must state two truthful facts and one lie about themselves to their teammates. The lie should be reasonable, nothing too outlandish. Then, within each team, figure out which statements were the lies AND then decide which teammate is the best liar.

Then, have the teams compete against each other by putting their best liars forward to determine the best liar in the room.

Team Naming - Active listening and communication

Randomly divide the participants into teams of size 3-4. Within each team, each member should introduce themselves and their hobbies, favorite activities, etc. to their team. Teams must then develop a name that captures their “essence" using positive, fun naming (do avoid inappropriate names - but if it happens, use it as a teachable moment).
