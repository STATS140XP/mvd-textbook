Locus of Control

“I am not a product of my circumstances. I am a product of my decisions." — Steven Covey

A General Definition of Locus of Control

Locus of control is a psychological concept that refers to how strongly people believe they have control over the situations and experiences that affect their lives. Locus means "place" or "location," and it pertains to where an individual perceives the source of control over events in their life.

There are two types of locus of control:

Internal Locus of Control:

This is when individuals believe they can control their life. They attribute success to their own efforts and abilities. They think that with hard work, skill, or intelligence, they can shape their future and overcome obstacles.

External Locus of Control:

In this case, individuals believe that their lives are controlled by external factors which they cannot influence. They may attribute outcomes to luck, fate, the economy, other people, or some higher power. They feel that their own actions make little difference in what happens to them.

Exercises

Small Group

Circle of Control

The goal of this exercise is to help consultants understand the difference between things they can control (internal locus of control) and things they cannot control (external locus of control).

Materials Needed:

  • Large sheets of paper or a whiteboard

  • Markers

  • Sticky notes (optional)

Procedure:

  1. Explain the concept of locus of control to the participants by describing the difference between an internal and external locus of control.

  2. Ask teams to draw two large circles on the paper or whiteboard, one inside the other and label the inner circle “Things I Can Control" and the outer circle”Things I Cannot Control."

  3. Ask each participant within a team to think of things in their life that they believe they can control (e.g., their attitude, effort, actions) and things they cannot control (e.g., other people’s actions, weather, past events). Participants can write these items on sticky notes or directly on the board.

  4. Ask participants to place their notes or write in the corresponding circles. Discuss as participants add their items. Encourage them to explain why they placed items in certain circles. Point out that focusing on the inner circle (things they can control) can empower them and reduce feelings of helplessness or frustration about external factors.

  5. Ask participants to reflect on how this exercise applies to their personal and professional lives. Encourage volunteers to share insights or personal experiences about recognizing what they can and cannot control.

  6. Summarize key takeaways from the activity. Emphasize the importance of focusing on areas within our control and how it can lead to more effective problem-solving and increased resilience.

Locus of control scale

For each question select the answer that you agree with the most.

Question one

I have learned that with putting in the expected effort, I will be considered an integral part of the team.

No matter how hard you try, it is not possible for me to connect with all team members.

Question two

I did not get the job because I did not know any key people in the company.

If I had done a better job preparing for the interview, I would have made the short list.

Question three

Less demanding and complicated projects are more likely to get high ratings.

Getting high rating on any type of project is the result of intelligent and continuing effort.

Question four

The famous quote by Thomas Jefferson: “I find that the harder I work, the more luck I seem to have” inspires me.

The famous quote by Orson Welles: “Nobody gets justice, people only get good luck or bad lack” inspires me.

Question five

People who practiced social distancing during COVID, did so because it was enforced by authorities.

People who were willing to practice social distancing during COVID, believe that their behavior has a positive relationship with their health.

Question six

With effort and collaboration, women can succeed in closing the gender gap in quantitative and technical fields.

In order to close the gender gap in science and technology fields, females have to be mentored and supported by men who are experts in the field.

Question seven

With making the right plan, putting in the necessary effort, and following through, any project will be successful.

When you are not lucky to have team members you can connect to and work with, the project will be disappointing.

Question eight

Success on multiple-choice tests depends more on your testing taking skills than your knowledge of the content.

When I do not know the right answer to some of the questions on a multiple choicetest, I know that picking the right answers randomly will not help me get a good grade.

Question nine

Effort and motivation are internal and stable while chance and luck are external and unstable.

In spite of being external and unstable, the role that luck can play in turning one’s life around is not comparable to effort.

Question ten

Individuals who believe that they are capable of achieving a goal, are less likely to attribute their success and failure to chance.

I know of individuals who did not work that hard, yet luck helped them become very successful.

Question eleven

The older members of my family believe that destiny predetermines the major events of one’s life and I tend to agree with them.

You cannot count on good luck. The only thing that helps with overcoming bad luck is hard work.

Question twelve

Majority of individuals who get to the top of the ladder know the right people and happen to be in the right place at the right time.

Majority of successful people are those who have the determination and invest the necessary effort to overcome bad luck.

Question thirteen

In order for women to get leadership roles in the work place, their technical and non-technical skills needs to be compatible with individuals in a similar positions.

In order for women to get leadership roles in the work place, they need to have each other’s back and have a strong relationship with key people.

Question fourteen

In the long run, the team members who take the leading role are responsible for the success of the project .

In the long run every member of the group is responsible for the success of the project.

Question fifteen

In the long run, the role of external circumstances on our failure and success is minimal.

In the long run, some of our projects in life fail and some succeed, it is like tossing of a coin.

Question sixteen

Successful parents are determined and work hard.

Successful parent are lucky to be born with better resources

Question seventeen

Some people are just lazy and wait for luck

Other people procrastinate and wait till the last minute.

Question eighteen

One should always be ready to admit poor planning

It is better to ignore poor planning.

Question nineteen

An effective instructor does anonymous surveys to find out what needs to be improved.

An effective instructor does not need to conduct anonymous surveys.

Question twenty

Having close friends is the best investment we can make.

Keeping family ties strong is the most important thing in life.
